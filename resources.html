Resources

With the suspension of SNAP benefits due to the ongoing government shutdown, nearly 272,000 Floridians — about one in seven people — are at risk of losing access to food assistance. This is not a minor disruption; it’s an emergency.

To meet the moment we must show up for one another in our community. JMAC is starting a weekly Meals in a Bag Initiative— a local, collective effort to pack shelf-stable ingredients and recipe cards into ready-to-cook meal kits. This is one among many mutual aid efforts to address the current crisis.
